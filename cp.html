Cookie Policy

This Cookie Policy explains how Typing Nova uses cookies and similar technologies to enhance your typing practice experience.

1. Introduction

Welcome to Typing Nova's Cookie Policy. This document explains what cookies are, how we use them on our typing practice website, and your choices regarding cookies.

At Typing Nova, we believe in being transparent about how we use technology to improve your typing practice sessions, speed tests, and overall website experience.

2. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer, smartphone, or other device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to website owners.

Think of cookies as a website's memory – they help the site remember your preferences, settings, and actions over time. For example, cookies might remember:

  • Your preferred typing test settings
  • Whether you've visited our site before
  • How you navigate through our typing exercises
  • Your progress in typing practice sessions

Important: Cookies are not programs and cannot run code. They cannot carry viruses or install malware on your device. They simply store information about your website visit.

3. How Typing Nova Uses Cookies

At Typing Nova, we use cookies for several important purposes to enhance your typing practice experience:

Types of Cookies We Use

Essential Cookies
These cookies are necessary for the website to function properly. They enable basic functions like page navigation and access to secure areas of the website. For example, they help maintain your typing test session so you don't lose your progress.
Preference Cookies
These cookies remember your choices and preferences, such as your preferred typing test duration (1, 3, or 5 minutes) or your favorite typing practice mode.
Analytics Cookies
These cookies help us understand how visitors use Typing Nova. They tell us which typing tests are most popular, how users navigate through our practice exercises, and which features need improvement.
Performance Cookies
These cookies help us improve the performance of Typing Nova by collecting information about how visitors interact with our typing practice tools.

We use this information to:

  • Improve our typing tests and practice exercises
  • Understand which features are most useful for typing practice
  • Fix technical issues with our typing speed tests
  • Make Typing Nova faster and more reliable
  • Create new typing practice features based on user needs

4. Google AdSense Cookies

Typing Nova uses Google AdSense to display advertisements. Google AdSense is a program that allows website owners to earn revenue by showing relevant ads to visitors.

Here's how Google AdSense cookies work on our site:

  • Google as a Third-Party Vendor: Google uses cookies to serve ads on Typing Nova based on your prior visits to our website and other sites on the internet.
  • DoubleClick Cookie: Google uses the DoubleClick cookie to enable it and its partners to serve ads based on your visit to Typing Nova and/or other sites on the internet.
  • Personalized Advertising: These cookies help show you ads that are more relevant to your interests. For example, if you frequently visit typing practice websites, you might see more ads related to typing tools or educational resources.
  • Non-Personalized Advertising: If you prefer not to see personalized ads, you can still use Typing Nova with non-personalized advertising.

Your Advertising Choices: You can opt out of personalized advertising from Google AdSense by visiting Google Ads Settings. Alternatively, you can opt out of third-party vendor's use of cookies for personalized advertising by visiting www.aboutads.info.

We do not control the ads served by Google AdSense and are not responsible for their content. However, we strive to ensure that all advertisements on Typing Nova are appropriate for a typing practice website and suitable for users of all ages.

5. Third-Party Cookies

In addition to our own cookies and Google AdSense cookies, we may also use various third-party cookies to enhance your experience on Typing Nova:

  • Google Analytics: We use Google Analytics to understand how users interact with our typing practice tools. This helps us improve our speed tests and passage typing exercises.
  • Content Delivery Networks (CDNs): We use CDNs to deliver our content faster to users around the world. These services may use cookies to optimize content delivery.
  • Font Services: We use Google Fonts to provide clean, readable typography for our typing exercises. These services may use cookies for performance optimization.

These third-party services have their own privacy policies and cookie practices. We encourage you to review their policies to understand how they use cookies:

  • Google Privacy & Terms
  • How Google uses information from sites that use its services

Please note that we do not have control over these third-party cookies. They are subject to the respective privacy policies of these external services.

6. Managing Your Cookie Preferences

You have control over cookies. Most web browsers allow you to manage your cookie preferences. You can choose to accept or reject cookies, and delete existing cookies from your device.

How to Manage Cookies in Your Browser:

  • Chrome: Settings → Privacy and security → Cookies and other site data
  • Firefox: Options → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Cookies and website data
  • Edge: Settings → Cookies and site permissions → Cookies and data stored

Important Note: If you choose to disable cookies on Typing Nova, some features of our typing practice website may not function properly. For example:

  • Your typing test preferences might not be saved
  • Your progress in typing practice sessions might not be remembered
  • You might need to reconfigure your typing test settings each time you visit
  • Some typing practice features might not work as expected

You can also use browser extensions or privacy tools to manage cookies more precisely. Many browsers offer "private" or "incognito" modes that limit cookie storage during your browsing session.

7. Your Typing Practice Data

It's important to understand the difference between cookies and your typing practice data:

  • Cookies: Small text files stored in your browser that help our website function
  • Typing Practice Data: Your typing speed test results, accuracy scores, and practice history

Your typing practice data (test results, speed measurements, accuracy percentages) is primarily stored locally in your browser's storage, not in cookies. This means:

  • Your typing progress is saved on your own device
  • We don't have access to your individual typing test results
  • You can clear this data anytime through your browser settings
  • This data helps you track your typing improvement over time

For more information about how we handle your data, please see our Privacy Policy.
